# Legal Notice Draft

This draft is for product prototyping and legal review. It is not legal advice. A production deployment should be reviewed by qualified counsel, hospital compliance teams, ethics committees, and information-security reviewers.

## Document Set

The access gate should include:

- Real-name registration and physician qualification commitment
- User agreement and platform rules
- Privacy and personal information notice
- Patient case, imaging, and sensitive information notice
- Multi-center research and ethics approval notice
- Medical-service boundary and disclaimer
- Violation handling, evidence retention, and accountability notice

## Draft Clauses

### Real-name registration and physician qualification commitment

Users must register with their real identity and submit true, complete, and valid physician license, professional title certificate, institution, and related qualification materials. A display name may be used in the platform, but real-name materials, credential files, and review records may be used for access review, account security, violation investigation, and accountability. Impersonation, credential sharing, falsification, concealment, expired credentials, or unauthorized use may lead to access denial, suspension, account termination, evidence preservation, and further legal accountability.

### Platform scope and medical-service boundary

The platform is designed for physician-to-physician calcaneal fracture case collection, imaging organization, teaching discussion, follow-up management, and research collaboration. It is not a patient-facing diagnosis or treatment service and does not provide prescriptions, medical orders, emergency support, autonomous diagnosis, or final surgical decision-making. Users remain responsible for their own lawful practice, hospital rules, medical quality and safety requirements, medical record obligations, and clinical judgment.

### Patient privacy, case material, and sensitive information protection

Case records, imaging, follow-up data, ID numbers, phone numbers, medical record numbers, inpatient numbers, face images, QR codes, examination numbers, exact dates, and rare identifying clues may constitute personal information or sensitive personal information. Before uploading, publishing, discussing, or including data in research, users should obtain the required authorization or ethics approval and complete de-identification. Public and discussion areas must not contain identifiable patient information. Doctor-local private follow-up data should be kept for the treating doctor's own follow-up and should not enter public cases, team discussions, or research datasets.

### Multi-center research and ethics approval

Cases included in multi-center research require ethics approval, institutional qualification materials, study-protocol authorization or consent documents, and compliance with data minimization, purpose limitation, access control, audit logging, withdrawal, and deletion mechanisms. Cases without ethics and qualification review should not be included in research datasets.

### Content rules and prohibited conduct

Users must not post unlawful, misleading, privacy-infringing, IP-infringing, impersonating, patient-soliciting, disguised advertising, out-of-scope practice, abusive, data-leaking, or system-abusing content. Sponsored content, device/tool promotion, and commercial cooperation must be clearly labeled and must not compromise the independence of clinical discussion or medical judgment.

### Violation handling, evidence retention, and accountability

If a user violates privacy protection, real-name qualification, medical ethics, platform security, research compliance, commercial disclosure, or other bottom-line rules, the platform may require correction, restrict functions, hide or delete content, suspend or terminate the account, preserve evidence, notify the user's institution, cooperate with regulators or judicial authorities, and pursue legal responsibility or compensation where appropriate.

## Legal Review Checklist

- Confirm whether the deployment is only an educational/research tool or involves regulated internet diagnosis/treatment.
- Confirm the required physician identity verification workflow and whether manual review is mandatory.
- Confirm patient authorization, de-identification, and ethics approval requirements for public teaching and multi-center research.
- Confirm cross-border data, cloud hosting, backup, and data processor obligations.
- Confirm retention periods, audit logs, deletion rights, consent withdrawal, and incident response.
- Confirm advertising and device/tool sales disclosures.
- Confirm whether any AI measurement, classification, or surgical-planning function may trigger medical-device or clinical decision-support requirements.

## Reference Areas For Counsel

The draft was shaped around these legal/compliance areas. Production counsel should verify the latest effective versions, local deployment jurisdiction, and hospital-specific requirements.

- Personal information and sensitive personal information protection, including medical health information, identity documents, phone numbers, and patient images.
- Real-name registration, account security, audit logging, and network/data security obligations.
- Physician qualification, lawful practice boundaries, and credential authenticity.
- Internet medical service boundaries, including whether a product is only education/research/case management or becomes internet diagnosis/treatment.
- Medical record, case material, imaging, and follow-up data management.
- Human-subject research, multi-center research, ethics approval, informed consent, data minimization, and research data governance.
- Advertising, commercial promotion, conflict-of-interest disclosure, and separation of commercial content from clinical discussion.
